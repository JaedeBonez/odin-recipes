Butternut Squash Soup

Fall is the perfect season for butternut squash soup. Especially when adding herbs like sage and rosemary into the mix, this soup hits on a whole other level. This soup is amazingly versatile, yet simple. The aroma that fills my kitchen when I find myself cooking this amazing dish is wonderfully fragrant. An added bonus is that one squash makes plenty of soup for a week, which makes this recipe a great option for easy and deliciously healthy meal prep!

The beauty of this recipe is how easy it is to modify to your personal tastes! While I personally wouldn't claim to be vegan, the recipe I will be sharing today will be entirely vegan. I have made this recipe many times since I found it and personally like to add some coconut/heavy cream, butter, chicken stock or more fragrant spices depending on the flavor I'm craving. However, I find is a good base to modify from so thats what I want to share with you here.

Equipment

  • Blender
  • Large pot

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 butternut squash around 3 pounds, peeled, de-seeded, and cut into one inch cubes
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • 1⁄2 tablespoon rosemary, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 3 to 4 cups vegetable broth
  • salt(to taste)
  • ground black pepper(to taste)

Optional toppings for serving

  • Chopped parsley
  • Toasted pepitas
  • Toasted bread

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, salt, and several grinds of fresh pepper and saut√© until soft, 5 to 8 minutes. Add the squash and cook until it begins to soften, stirring occasionally, for 8 to 10 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, sage, rosemary, and ginger. Stir and cook 30 seconds to 1 minute, until fragrant, then add 3 cups of the broth. Bring to a boil, cover, and reduce heat to a simmer. Cook until the squash is tender, 20 to 30 minutes.
  3. Let cool slightly and pour the soup into a blender, working in batches if necessary, and blend until smooth. If your soup is too thick, add up to 1 cup more broth and blend. Season to taste and serve with parsley, pepitas, and crusty bread.
